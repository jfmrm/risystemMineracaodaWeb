<h1 class="title mathjax"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
Dynamic Modeling, Stability, and Control of Power Systems with  Distributed Energy Resources</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Sadamoto%2C+T">Tomonori Sadamoto</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Chakrabortty%2C+A">Aranya Chakrabortty</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Ishizaki%2C+T">Takayuki Ishizaki</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Imura%2C+J">Jun-ichi Imura</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 13 Apr 2018 (<a href="/abs/1804.04933v1">v1</a>), last revised 13 Sep 2018 (this version, v3))</div>
<blockquote class="abstract mathjax">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> This article presents a suite of new control designs for next-generation
electric smart grids. The future grid will consist of thousands of
non-conventional renewable generation sources such as wind, solar, and energy
storage. These new components are collectively referred to as distributed
energy resources (DER). The article presents a comprehensive list of dynamic
models for DERs, and shows their coupling with the conventional generators and
loads. It then presents several innovative control designs that can be used for
facilitating large-scale DER integration. Ideas from decentralized retrofit
control and distributed sparsity-promoting optimal control are used for
developing these designs, followed by illustrations on an IEEE power system
test model.
</blockquote>
