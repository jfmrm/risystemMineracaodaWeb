<h1 class="title mathjax"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
Dispersion Bound for the Wyner-Ahlswede-K&#xf6;rner Network via Reverse  Hypercontractivity on Types</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/search/cs,math?searchtype=author&query=Liu%2C+J">Jingbo Liu</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 9 May 2018 (<a href="/abs/1805.03367v1">v1</a>), last revised 12 Sep 2018 (this version, v2))</div>
<blockquote class="abstract mathjax">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> This paper introduces a new converse machinery for a challenging class of
distributed source-type problems (e.g.\ distributed source coding, common
randomness generation, or hypothesis testing with communication constraints),
through the example of the Wyner-Ahlswede-K\"orner network. Using the
functional-entropic duality and the reverse hypercontractivity of the
transposition semigroup, we lower bound the error probability for each joint
type. Then by averaging the error probability over types, we lower bound the
$c$-dispersion (which characterizes the second-order behavior of the weighted
sum of the rates of the two compressors when a nonvanishing error probability
is small) as the variance of the gradient of $\inf_{P_{U|X}}\{cH(Y|U)+I(U;X)\}$
with respect to $Q_{XY}$, the per-letter side information and source
distribution. In comparison, using standard achievability arguments based on
the method of types, we upper-bound the $c$-dispersion as the variance of
$c\imath_{Y|U}(Y|U)+\imath_{U;X}(U;X)$, which improves the existing upper
bounds but has a gap to the aforementioned lower bound.
</blockquote>
