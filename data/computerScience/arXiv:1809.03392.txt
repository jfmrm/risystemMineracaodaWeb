<h1 class="title mathjax"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
Optimal Partition of a Tree with Social Distance</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Okubo%2C+M">Masahiro Okubo</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Hanaka%2C+T">Tesshu Hanaka</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Ono%2C+H">Hirotaka Ono</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 10 Sep 2018 (<a href="/abs/1809.03392v1">v1</a>), last revised 18 Sep 2018 (this version, v2))</div>
<blockquote class="abstract mathjax">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> We study the problem to find a partition of graph $G$ with maximum social
welfare based on social distance between vertices in $G$, called {\sf MaxSWP}.
This problem is known to be NP-hard in general. In this paper, we first give a
complete characterization of optimal partitions of trees with small diameters
based on analytical analyses. Then, by utilizing the results, we present {\sf
MaxSWP} of a tree can be solved in linear time. Moreover, we show that {\sf
MaxSWP} is NP-hard even for 4-regular graphs.
</blockquote>
