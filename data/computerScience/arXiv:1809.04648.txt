<h1>Computer Science > Cryptography and Security</h1>

<h1 class="title mathjax"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
Reversing the asymmetry in data exfiltration</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Skillicorn%2C+D">David Skillicorn</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Li%2C+X">Xiao Li</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Chen%2C+K">Karen Chen</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 12 Sep 2018)</div>
<blockquote class="abstract mathjax">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> Preventing data exfiltration from computer systems typically depends on
perimeter defences, but these are becoming increasingly fragile. Instead we
suggest an approach in which each at-risk document is supplemented by many fake
versions of itself. An attacker must either exfiltrate all of them; or try to
discover which is the real one while operating within the penetrated system,
and both are difficult. Creating and maintaining many fakes is relatively
inexpensive, so the advantage that typically accrues to an attacker now lies
with the defender. We show that algorithmically generated fake documents are
reasonably difficult to detect using algorithmic analytics.
</blockquote>
