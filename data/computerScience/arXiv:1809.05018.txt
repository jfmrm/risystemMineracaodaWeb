<h1 class="title mathjax"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
DPP-PMRF: Rethinking Optimization for a Probabilistic Graphical Model  Using Data-Parallel Primitives</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Lessley%2C+B">Brenton Lessley</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Perciano%2C+T">Talita Perciano</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Heinemann%2C+C">Colleen Heinemann</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Camp%2C+D">David Camp</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Childs%2C+H">Hank Childs</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Bethel%2C+E+W">E. Wes Bethel</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 13 Sep 2018)</div>
<blockquote class="abstract mathjax">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> We present a new parallel algorithm for probabilistic graphical model
optimization. The algorithm relies on data-parallel primitives (DPPs), which
provide portable performance over hardware architecture. We evaluate results on
CPUs and GPUs for an image segmentation problem. Compared to a serial baseline,
we observe runtime speedups of up to 13X (CPU) and 44X (GPU). We also compare
our performance to a reference, OpenMP-based algorithm, and find speedups of up
to 7X (CPU).
</blockquote>
