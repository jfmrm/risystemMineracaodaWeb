<h1 class="title mathjax"><span class="descriptor">Title:</span>
Nanopublications: A Growing Resource of Provenance-Centric Scientific  Linked Data</h1>
<div class="authors"><span class="descriptor">Authors:</span>
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Kuhn%2C+T">Tobias Kuhn</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Mero%C3%B1o-Pe%C3%B1uela%2C+A">Albert Mero&#xf1;o-Pe&#xf1;uela</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Malic%2C+A">Alexander Malic</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Poelen%2C+J+H">Jorrit H. Poelen</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Hurlbert%2C+A+H">Allen H. Hurlbert</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Ortiz%2C+E+C">Emilio Centeno Ortiz</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Furlong%2C+L+I">Laura I. Furlong</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Queralt-Rosinach%2C+N">N&#xfa;ria Queralt-Rosinach</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Chichester%2C+C">Christine Chichester</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Banda%2C+J+M">Juan M. Banda</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Willighagen%2C+E">Egon Willighagen</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Ehrhart%2C+F">Friederike Ehrhart</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Evelo%2C+C">Chris Evelo</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Malas%2C+T+B">Tareq B. Malas</a>, 
<a href="/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Dumontier%2C+M">Michel Dumontier</a></div>
<div class="dateline">(Submitted on 18 Sep 2018)</div>
<blockquote class="abstract mathjax">
<span class="descriptor">Abstract:</span> Nanopublications are a Linked Data format for scholarly data publishing that
has received considerable uptake in the last few years. In contrast to the
common Linked Data publishing practice, nanopublications work at the granular
level of atomic information snippets and provide a consistent container format
to attach provenance and metadata at this atomic level. While the
nanopublications format is domain-independent, the datasets that have become
available in this format are mostly from Life Science domains, including data
about diseases, genes, proteins, drugs, biological pathways, and biotic
interactions. More than 10 million such nanopublications have been published,
which now form a valuable resource for studies on the domain level of the given
Life Science domains as well as on the more technical levels of provenance
modeling and heterogeneous Linked Data. We provide here an overview of this
combined nanopublication dataset, show the results of some overarching
analyses, and describe how it can be accessed and queried.
</blockquote>
