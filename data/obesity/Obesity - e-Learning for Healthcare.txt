<h2>About the Obesity programme</h2>
<p><a href="/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/Obesity_Image2.jpg"><img class="alignright size-full wp-image-6276" src="/wp-content/uploads/2018/05/Obesity_Image2.jpg" alt="Obesity_Image2" /></a>Obesity is an engaging e-learning programme for practitioners in the NHS and local authorities working in weight management.</p>
<p>It was developed by the Department of Health Obesity Team in partnership with Health Education England e-Learning for Healthcare in 2010, and reviewed and updated in partnership with Public Health England in 2017.</p>
<p>The programme introduces obesity and its implications for health, provides a knowledge base on identification of unhealthy weight and risk factors for weight gain, managing obesity, and learning on guiding and enabling behaviour change.</p>
<p>The learning is relevant for a wide audience, including doctors, particularly foundation doctors, GP trainees and GPs, nurses and any NHS or local authority practitioner who encounters overweight or obese individuals.</p>

<h2>How to access</h2>
<p>In order to access any e-LfH programme, you will need an e-LfH account. If you do not have one, then you can register by selecting the Register button below.</p>
<p><a class="normallink" href="http://portal.e-lfh.org.uk/Register" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Register &gt;</a></p>
<p>If you already have an account with e-LfH, then you can enrol on to the Obesity programme by logging in to the e-LfH Hub, selecting <strong>My Account</strong> &gt; <strong>Enrolment</strong> and selecting the programme. You can then access the programme immediately in the <strong>My e-Learning</strong> section.</p>
<h4>NHS healthcare staff in England</h4>
<p>The Obesity programme is also available to NHS healthcare staff via the Electronic Staff Record (ESR). Accessing this <a href="https://www.electronicstaffrecord.nhs.uk/esr-operational/functionality/esr-e-learning/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">e-Learning via ESR</a> means that your completions will transfer with you throughout your NHS career.</p>
<p>Further details are available <a href="http://www.esrsupport.co.uk/nlms/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">here</a>.</p>
