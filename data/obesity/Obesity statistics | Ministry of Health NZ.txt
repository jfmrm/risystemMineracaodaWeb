            <span property="dc:title" content="Obesity statistics" class="rdf-meta element-hidden"></span>      </header>
    <div class="field field-name-field-intro-text field-type-text-long field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even"><p class="intro-text">This page provides introductory statistics about obesity in New Zealand as well as links to statistical publications.</p></div></div></div><div class="field field-name-body field-type-text-with-summary field-label-hidden"><div class="field-items"><div class="field-item even" property="content:encoded"><h2>Adult obesity statistics</h2>

<p>The New Zealand Health Survey 2016/17 found that:</p>

<ul>
	<li>around 1 in 3 adults (aged 15 years and over) were obese (32%)</li>
	<li>a further 34% of adults were overweight but not obese</li>
	<li>50% of Māori adults were obese</li>
	<li>69% of Pacific adults were obese</li>
	<li>adults living in the most deprived areas were 1.5 times as likely to be obese as adults living in the least deprived areas<a href="#footnote">*</a></li>
	<li>the adult obesity rate increased from 27% in 2006/07 to 32% in 2016/17.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Child obesity statistics</h2>

<p>The New Zealand Health Survey 2016/17 found that:</p>

<ul>
	<li>around 1 in 8 children (aged 2&ndash;14 years) were obese (12%)</li>
	<li>a further 21% were children were overweight but not obese</li>
	<li>18% of Māori children were obese</li>
	<li>29% of Pacific children were obese</li>
	<li>children living in the most deprived areas were 2.5 times as likely to be obese as children living in the least deprived areas<a href="#footnote">*</a></li>
	<li>the child obesity rate increased from 8% in 2006/07 to 12% in 2016/17.</li>
</ul>

<p>For more information refer to the <a href="https://minhealthnz.shinyapps.io/nz-health-survey-2016-17-annual-data-explorer/">Annual Data Explorer: New Zealand Health Survey 2016/17</a>.</p>

<hr />
<p><a name="footnote">*</a> Adjusted for differences in age, sex and ethnicity.</p>
